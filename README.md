The Problem When a crime happens, evidence gets lost, altered, or dismissed. A victim records a video — but how do you prove it wasn't edited? How do you prove it was recorded at that exact moment, at that exact location? Today you can't. Courts require a chain of custody that most digital evidence simply cannot provide. The Solution: Immutable Evidence on a Public Blockchain GIBBOR is a panic button. The moment someone activates it: Their GPS coordinates + timestamp are cryptographically hashed and written to the Stellar blockchain via a smart contract — permanently, in under 30 seconds, for fractions of a cent. The phone starts recording audio and video automatically — no action required from a potentially endangered person. When recording stops, the SHA-256 fingerprint of that file is anchored on-chain. The file stays on the device; only its mathematical fingerprint goes to the blockchain. Why Each Technology Exists Stellar + Soroban Smart Contract Stellar is a public, permission-less blockchain that finalizes transactions in 3–5 seconds. The smart contract stores: who triggered the incident, when, where, and the hash of the evidence. Nobody — not even GIBBOR — can alter or delete that record. It's a public timestamp with legal weight. GoldSky Raw blockchain data is difficult to query. GoldSky indexes Stellar smart contract events in real time and exposes them as a SQL-queryable API. This means law enforcement, lawyers, or a judge can pull up every incident linked to a user, verify the on-chain timestamps, and cross-reference the evidence hashes — without needing to understand blockchain at all. SHA-256 Hashing The video file never leaves the phone unless the user chooses to share it. What goes on-chain is a 64-character fingerprint. If anyone later claims the video was edited, you run the same SHA-256 function on the file and compare. If the hash matches what's on the blockchain, the file is provably unaltered. If it doesn't match — the tampering is proven. Crossmint Normally, interacting with a blockchain requires the user to manage a crypto wallet, private keys, and gas fees — a barrier that kills adoption. Crossmint abstracts all of that. The user signs in with their institutional email. Crossmint creates and manages a Stellar wallet invisibly. The user never sees a seed phrase or buys crypto.
